# Key Findings
1. **Definitions**:
   - **Naive RAG** integrates external data retrieval into the generation process, enhancing language models.
   - **Sentence-Window Retrieval** focuses on retrieving text at the sentence level, providing precise context.

2. **Advantages and Disadvantages**:
   - **Naive RAG**:
     - Advantages: Reduces retraining costs, improves accuracy, enhances transparency.
     - Disadvantages: May produce incorrect answers if context is misaligned.
   - **Sentence-Window Retrieval**:
     - Advantages: High precision, especially in specialized fields.
     - Disadvantages: Computationally intensive and less effective in broader contexts.

3. **Performance**:
   - Sentence-window retrieval generally outperforms naive RAG in accuracy and relevance, particularly in detailed context tasks.

4. **Use Cases**:
   - Naive RAG is suitable for dynamic information retrieval applications, while sentence-window retrieval is ideal for high-accuracy tasks like legal and medical analysis.
