### Revised Summary Report on Keyboard Layouts

1. **Common Keyboard Layouts**:
   - The most prevalent keyboard layout is **QWERTY**, designed in the 1870s to prevent typewriter jamming. Alternatives include **AZERTY** (primarily in French-speaking regions) and **Dvorak**, which aims to enhance typing speed and reduce finger movement by placing frequently used letters under the strongest fingers.

2. **History of Keyboard Layouts**:
   - QWERTY emerged with the first commercially successful typewriter in the 1870s. Dvorak was introduced in the 1930s, claiming to improve typing efficiency. Despite Dvorak's potential advantages, QWERTY remains dominant due to its widespread adoption and user familiarity (Source: ThoughtCo / History of the Computer Keyboard / Relevance: High).

3. **QWERTY vs. AZERTY vs. Dvorak**:
   - QWERTY is standard in English-speaking countries, while AZERTY is tailored for French. Dvorak is designed for efficiency but has not gained significant traction due to the inertia of QWERTY's established user base. Studies indicate that while Dvorak may offer speed advantages, the difference is often marginal for casual typists (Source: Das Keyboard / QWERTY vs. Dvorak vs. Colemak / Relevance: High).

4. **Keyboard Layout Preferences by Region**:
   - Preferences vary significantly by region, with QWERTY dominating in the US and many English-speaking countries, while AZERTY is prevalent in France and Belgium. Dvorak has a niche following among enthusiasts. As of 2026, fewer than 1% of typists use alternative layouts like Dvorak or Colemak, indicating a strong preference for QWERTY due to familiarity and ease of use (Source: Metatyping Club / Keyboard Layouts Explained / Relevance: High).

5. **User Experiences and Comparisons**:
   - Users transitioning to Dvorak report an initial learning curve of 60-80 hours to reach previous QWERTY speeds, with an additional 40-60 hours to exceed them. Many users find the ergonomic benefits of Dvorak appealing, but the effort required to switch often deters casual typists. In 2026, keyboard layouts are increasingly tailored to specific user needs, balancing connectivity, comfort, and customization (Source: Dock Universe / Best Keyboard Layouts for 2026 / Relevance: High).
