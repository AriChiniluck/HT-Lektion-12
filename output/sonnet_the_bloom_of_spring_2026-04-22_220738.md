# Sonnet: The Bloom of Spring

In spring's embrace, the flowers start to wake,  
With petals soft, like whispers in the breeze,  
They dance beneath the sun, a vibrant flake,  
In colors bright, they paint the world with ease.  

The daffodils, in golden hues, arise,  
While tulips stand in rows, a regal sight,  
Each bloom a promise, under azure skies,  
A symphony of life, a pure delight.  

Yet fleeting is their beauty, soft and sweet,  
As time will steal their charm with summer's heat,  
But in their brief display, hope finds its place,  
A reminder that in change, we find grace.  

So let us cherish blooms, both bold and shy,  
For in their transient life, we learn to fly.
